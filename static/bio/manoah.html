Manoah Kefa William

Education and Early Childhood Development Programme Senior Manager, BRAC International

About Manoah Kefa William:

Manoah Kefa William is an accomplished professional in the Early Childhood Development (ECD) sector, possessing over a decade of experience, with 8 years of direct involvement in ECD initiatives. He has overseen and managed numerous impactful programs, particularly in Tanzania between 2017 and 2021, where he led major projects that included establishing 30 satellite pre-primary classes and constructing and opening 35 ECD centers (“play labs”) in government primary schools in Tanga.

Manoah's work also featured the creation and management of 55 study centers for out-of-school girls (“ALP Centers”), in partnership with the Institute of Adult Education (IAE). His initiatives further extended to the development of the ECD Radio program, *MALEZI na BRAC* (Parenting with BRAC), in collaboration with Tanzania Broadcasting Cooperation (TBC). Additionally, he served as a Technical Advisor for ECD in South Sudan.

In his current role as Senior Manager for the Education and Early Childhood Development Programme at BRAC International, Manoah oversees ECD and education programs across multiple countries. He is heavily involved in program design, fundraising, and implementation, working closely with country teams and collaborating with governments to develop national ECD documents and protocols. His notable achievements include the development of the National Caseworkers Guide for Tanzania and leading the creation of the Sierra Leone ECD Teacher’s Guide for children aged 3-6.

Manoah also has significant expertise in youth and women programming, demonstrated by his leadership of the EU-funded Community Rise Programme in South Sudan, which supported youth empowerment and women's income-generating activities. In Tanzania, he is an active member of the National ECD Taskforce and has contributed to developing the ECD Care Workers Guide, as well as reviewing ECD models alongside partners such as UNICEF and other government bodies.

Fluent in Swahili and English, Manoah is known for his strong writing, analytical, and communication skills, crucial for managing complex programs and initiatives. He holds a Bachelor of Education and is pursuing a Master’s in Education Management and Administration at the University of Dar es Salaam.
