Adv. Anna Kulaya

National Coordinator, WILDAF

Professional Background:

Adv. Anna Kulaya has devoted 20 years to serving the Tanzanian community through her extensive work in the legal sector, advocacy, governance, gender, and development. She has substantial expertise in program design, strategic planning, implementation management, community mobilization, and gender-transformative initiatives.

Ms. Kulaya has played a pivotal role in national campaigns to raise awareness about gender-based violence (GBV), such as the 16 Days of Activism against Gender-Based Violence Campaign. She has also been an essential member of the steering and technical committees for the National Plan of Action to End Violence against Women and Children (NPA-VAWC) for the past nine years and contributed significantly to the enactment of the Legal Aid Act of 2017 to aid GBV victims in accessing justice.

Her work includes championing paralegal and legal aid services for marginalized citizens, particularly GBV victims. She has facilitated the establishment of Police Gender and Children Desks (PGCD) and influenced the integration of GBV programs into police training curricula.

Ms. Kulaya is a trained facilitator on sexual harassment, exploitation, and abuse, having worked with over 100 industries in Tanzania to develop related policies. She also trains health workers on GBV response using the GBV Manual from the Ministry of Health and leverages the SASA! Approach for community GBV training.

She is a social scientist and researcher trained in GBV and sexual abuse by the International Center on Research for Women and Muhimbili University of Health and Allied Sciences. With a Master of Laws (LLM) in Migration and Refugee Law and a Bachelor of Laws (LLB) from the University of Dar Es Salaam, Ms. Kulaya is a dedicated advocate committed to ending GBV against women and girls.
