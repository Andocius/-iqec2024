Tike Mwambipile

Executive Director, TAWLA

About Tike Mwambipile:

Ms. Tike Mwambipile is the Executive Director of the Tanzania Women Lawyers Association (TAWLA). She holds an LLB (Hons) and an MBA from Tanzania, along with a postgraduate diploma in economic diplomacy.

Adv. Tike has 17 years of experience across various fields, including women and children's rights, legal and human rights issues, gender, sexual and reproductive health (SRH&R), program development and management, fundraising, donor relations, gender governance, research and advocacy, facilitation, and documentation.

Throughout her career, Tike has worked with civil society organizations at grassroots, national, and international levels. She is known for her strong leadership skills, training, research, fundraising, and organizational management. Her direct, hands-on approach is supported by excellent written and oral communication abilities.

Ms. Mwambipile has conducted numerous legal research projects aimed at influencing policy and legislative review to promote the rights of women, children, and youth. Her advocacy work has consistently demonstrated her commitment to these groups, both within Tanzania and on an international stage. Tike is passionate about supporting the marginalized, often spending significant time in the field engaging with and supporting poor communities.

She has served on various boards and councils in different capacities, including:

  • Honorary Secretary of Tanzania Gender Accountability (current)
  • Chairperson of Sand for Her Land Campaign (S4HL) (current)
  • Chairperson of Tanzania End Child Marriage Network (TECMN) (current)
  • Secretary of the Executive Council of TAWLA
  • Board Member of Tanzania Human Rights Defenders (2012-2015)

In 2018, through her engagement with the government, Tike was appointed by the Ministry of Land to sit on the National Land Advisory Council from 2018 to 2021.
