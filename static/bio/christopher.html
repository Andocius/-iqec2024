Brief Profile:

Christopher Peter holds a Bachelor's Degree in Rural Development from Sokoine University of Agriculture (2013) and is currently a Project Officer at the Tanzania Early Childhood Development Network (TECDEN). With over 9 years of experience in Early Childhood Development (ECD) and education, he has successfully led and implemented numerous ECD projects across Tanzania, focusing on Early Childhood Development for children aged 0 to 8 years.

Christopher has completed notable courses, including the Science of Early Childhood Development (Aga Khan University, 2016), a Massive Open Online Course (MOOC) on Mainstreaming ECE into Education Sector Planning (IIEP-UNESCO/UNICEF, 2019), and Leadership for Sustainable Collaboration Networks (2024). As a National Facilitator for the National Multi-Sectoral ECD Programme 2021/22-2025/26, he collaborates with government entities and organizations like Children in Crossfire and the Union of Tanzania Press Clubs (UTPC).

At the national level, Christopher is involved in shaping policies that impact children, working with MDAs such as the President’s Office – Regional Administration and Local Government (PORALG), the Ministry of Community Development, Gender, Women, and Special Groups (MOCDGWSG), the Ministry of Education Science and Technology (MoEST), the Prime Minister’s Office (PMO), and the Ministry of Health (MOH). He played a key role as a member of the secretariat of the organizing committee for the Regional Early Childhood Development Conference in March 2024. He has also contributed to the development of pre-primary learning materials and facilitated ECD training across multiple regions. In 2024, he served as a Master Trainer for pre-primary teacher training in Simiyu region under MOEST and participated in the preparation of Pre-primary Classrooms Model in Morogoro and Geita during Global Education Week 2023 and 2024, respectively.
