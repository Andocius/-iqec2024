Clemence Nhilizyo

Policy and Advocacy Manager, CAMFED Zimbabwe

About Clemence Nhilizyo:

Clemence Nhilizyo is a seasoned policy and advocacy expert with 16 years of specialized experience in education, land, and agrarian policies. In his role as Programs Manager for the Education Coalition of Zimbabwe, he spearheaded critical advocacy efforts in education policy, working closely with government officials to advance educational reforms.

He played a pivotal role in coordinating public consultations for the Education Amendment Act (No. 15; 2019) in Zimbabwe, collaborating with civil society, government bodies, parliament, and development partners. His extensive consultancy work includes impactful projects with the World Bank Group, assisting the Ministry of Primary and Secondary Education in formulating the ICT in Education Policy to promote blended learning and educational technology integration.

Clemence has contributed to key research projects, such as "Improving Teacher Professionalization and Well-being through Child-Centred Pedagogical Coaching in Ghana" for World Vision Canada and World Vision Ghana (2024), and the "Geographic Inquiry for Girls' Education Programming in a Conflict-Affected Context" in SÃ©gou & Mopti, Mali (2023).

His in-depth knowledge of the Global Partnership for Education (GPE) processes is evident in his work on the GPE-Zimbabwe 2022-2026 Partnership Compact and his involvement in multiple grant selection committees. As the Policy and Advocacy Manager at CAMFED Zimbabwe, Clemence currently oversees the GPE Girls Education Accelerator (GEA) Programme, which aims to advance girls' education in the region.

Clemence's extensive experience and leadership continue to make a significant impact on educational policy and advocacy initiatives across Zimbabwe.
