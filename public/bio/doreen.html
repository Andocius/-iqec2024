Doreen Michael

Youth Advisory Panel - UNFPA

About Doreen Michael:

Doreen Michael is a tech enthusiast and advocate at the intersection of gender equity, youth development, and climate change, with a strong focus on ocean conservation and biodiversity. She is currently a member of the UNFPA Tanzania Youth Advisory Council and collaborates with the Tanzania Youth Climate Coalition (TYCC) to engage youth in climate action.

In 2019, Doreen founded Renotify, a digital platform that has supported over 1,000 students across Tanzania. She has also developed educational curricula aimed at creating inclusive opportunities for women in STEAM fields, directly impacting 1,500 women and contributing to policy revisions that positively influenced 12,000 individuals.

Doreen represented Tanzania at the SEAL Heirs to Our Ocean Summit in 2020, where she produced a film raising awareness about the impact of sewage and waste management on ocean pollution. Her work with National Geographic and The Nature Conservancy has showcased her dedication to environmental conservation, focusing on endangered marine species and promoting intergenerational action.

She has been an active participant at events like COP28 alongside YOUNGO and has organized local actions to engage youth. Doreen's work reflects her belief in empowering young women to tackle societal challenges, using technology to amplify awareness and inspire change.

Her expertise lies in STEAM, gender equity, conservation (biodiversity, marine, and wildlife). She continues to champion climate justice, youth empowerment, and gender equality with a deep commitment to making impactful change.
