Mwajuma Kibwana

Executive Director, Tanzania Early Childhood Development Network

About Mwajuma Kibwana:

Mwajuma Kibwana (M.A. in Development Studies) has served as the Executive Director of the Tanzania Early Childhood Development Network (TECDEN) since January 2021. She brings 18 years of experience across various programs, including HIV/AIDS, youth and women empowerment, accountability, and early childhood development (ECD).

In her current role, Mwajuma provides strategic leadership and technical guidance to ensure that TECDEN’s programs align with the network’s objectives. She also works to create and maintain strategic partnerships within and beyond the African region, fostering growth and collaboration.

Over the past three years, Mwajuma has successfully led TECDEN’s coordination of ECD non-state actors, promoting collaborative efforts with the Tanzanian government to advance the ECD agenda. This work has included the development, launch, and implementation of the National Multi-sectoral ECD Program (NM-ECDP) at both national and sub-national levels.

Mwajuma is recognized as a Global Leader for Young Children under the mentorship of the World Forum Foundation. Fluent in Swahili and English, she continues to contribute significantly to early childhood development in Tanzania.
