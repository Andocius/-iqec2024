Arafat Lesheve

Founder of Tanzania Youth Awards

About Arafat Lesheve:

Mr. Arafat Lesheve is an experienced institutional strategic young leader committed to empowering and supporting young Africans to drive development and achieve the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and Agenda 2063.

With over eight years of extensive experience in Project Design and Management, Advocacy, Institutional Capacity Development, Resource Mobilization, and Strategic Partnership, Arafat has shown proven expertise across diverse sectors including NGOs, civil society, youth networks, TVET education institutions, government bodies, and international organizations.

He currently serves as a Youth Engagement and Project Management Consultant and is the Co-Founder and Director of the Emerging Generation Initiative, a youth-led organization that empowers youth, advocates for youth agendas, and organizes the annual Tanzania Emerging Youth Awards.

Arafat's work spans national, regional, and international levels with a focus on community, adolescent, youth, and gender-focused development programs. His expertise encompasses areas such as livelihoods, TVET education, sexual and reproductive health, WASH, menstrual hygiene health, climate change, labor relations, civic engagement, employability and entrepreneurship, and 21st-century skills development.

Throughout his career, Arafat has dedicated himself to humanitarian work and advocacy, empowering young individuals and institutional systems to align with the SDGs and Agenda 2063. He has raised awareness and trained over 30,000 young Africans and community members on SDG-related topics, economic empowerment, climate change, COVID-19 leadership, sexual and reproductive health, and more.

In addition, Arafat has played a role in supporting the Tanzanian government with the development of the National Youth Development Policy and youth engagement strategies for various NGOs. He has also contributed through research and consultancy in areas such as SDG implementation, gender analysis, child labor, project evaluation, and labor market analysis, all aiding evidence-based decision-making and impactful program design.
